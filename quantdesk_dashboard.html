🎮 Game Theory Analysis
Models market dynamics as a 3-player game between institutional investors, retail traders, and market makers. Uses two frameworks:
• NASH Equilibrium: Dominant strategy where no player benefits from unilateral change (e.g., all BUY, all SELL, or mixed).
• Prisoner's Dilemma: Payoff analysis where "Cooperate" (accumulate) vs "Defect" (distribute). When mutual payoffs favor cooperation, expect BUY signals.
Scores 60+ = Bullish alignment, 40-60 = Divergence, <40 = Bearish consensus.

⚙️ Prisoner's Dilemma — Payoff Analysis
Each player chooses to "Cooperate" (accumulate) or "Defect" (distribute). Payoffs: R = mutual cooperation reward → High price · T = betrayal temptation when other cooperates → Quick breakout · S = sucker loss when you cooperate, other defects → Liquidation risk · P = mutual defection punishment → Drawn-out decline. Chart shows when rational players choose to cooperate (BUY) or defect (SELL).
